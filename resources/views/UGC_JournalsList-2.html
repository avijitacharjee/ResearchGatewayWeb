Plagiarism means 'wrongful appropriation' or 'close imitation' of an author's 'language, thoughts, ideas or expressions'. This can happen without intention. There are various guidelines that universities prescribe for citation of work, whenever you are referring to someone else's work or using a definition. Making sure that you have the right citation and format style is essential to save your dissertation from getting plagiarized.


Do not be stopped in your journey of creating a dissertation because of plagiarized content, which can also lead to rejection. Instead, get the help of a service for ensuring the authenticity of your work, as well as a positive response from peers and professors. Proving that your work is original and defending it is a crucial step in the process of evaluation of your work.
